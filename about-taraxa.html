About Taraxa

Taraxa is a high-performance public ledger designed to power the next generation of real-world decentralized applications. Unlike many blockchains that struggle with the speed, cost, and scale required for everyday use, Taraxa is purpose-built to handle the massive volume of small, frequent transactions generated by IoT devices, data streams, and machine-to-machine interactions.

At its core, Taraxa utilizes a blockDAG (Directed Acyclic Graph) consensus mechanism. This advanced architecture allows for highly concurrent transaction processing, resulting in incredibly fast speeds, near-instant finality, and extremely low fees. Crucially, Taraxa is also fully EVM-compatible, empowering developers to build with the familiar tools and smart contracts of the vast Ethereum ecosystem without sacrificing performance.

What truly sets Taraxa apart is its specific focus on enabling the Machine-to-Machine (M2M) economy. For autonomous devices to interact and transact at scale, the underlying network must be both exceptionally fast and economically viable for countless micro-transactions. Taraxa’s architecture is engineered to meet these precise demands, providing the trustless and efficient infrastructure required for machines to exchange value and data without human intermediaries.

Ultimately, Taraxa is building the foundational layer for a more decentralized and automated future. By providing the reliable and scalable rails for the emerging M2M economy, Taraxa empowers projects like Robot Shares to move from concept to reality. It is not just another blockchain—it is a fundamental building block for the autonomous world of tomorrow.
